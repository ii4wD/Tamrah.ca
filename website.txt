  <section id="blood-sugar" aria-labelledby="blood-h">
    <h2 id="blood-h">Blood sugar, insulin & energy</h2>
    <div class="two">
      <div class="card">
        <h3>What the evidence says</h3>
        <ul>
          <li>Across studies, dates generally fall in the <em>low-to-medium</em> GI range, depending on variety and serving size.</li>
          <li>In moderation, dates <em>have not been shown to worsen glycemic control</em> in people with type 2 diabetes ‚Äî likely thanks to their fiber and polyphenols.</li>
          <li>Compared with refined sugar, whole dates tend to produce a steadier curve (less ‚Äúsugar crash‚Äù), especially when paired with protein/fat.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="callout" style="margin-top:10px"><strong>Practical tip:</strong> A mindful snack is 2 large or 3 small dates with nuts, yogurt, or cheese ‚Äî fiber + fat/protein = smoother energy.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <h3>Make it work for you</h3>
        <ul>
          <li>Choose whole dates over syrups.</li>
          <li>Pair with protein/fat (nuts, tahini) and consider smaller portions before long sedentary periods.</li>
          <li>If you track glucose, test your response ‚Äî varieties and bodies differ.</li>
        </ul>
        <p style="font-size:13px;color:var(--muted)">This is nutrition guidance, not medical advice. If you manage diabetes, follow your clinician‚Äôs plan.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="varieties" aria-labelledby="varieties-h">
    <h2 id="varieties-h">Popular varieties</h2>
    <div class="grid-3">
      <article class="feature">
        <h3>Medjool</h3>
        <p>Large, very soft, caramel-like. Often used as a natural sweetener; typically low-to-medium GI.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="feature">
        <h3>Sukkari</h3>
        <p>Golden and delicate with a sweet crunch near the crown when fresher; prized in Saudi Arabia.</p>
      </article>
      <article class="feature">
        <h3>Khalas</h3>
        <p>Balanced sweetness, soft-sticky texture; excellent with Arabic coffee.</p>
      </article>
    </div>
    <p style="color:var(--muted);font-size:13px;margin-top:8px">Taste/texture vary by harvest and curing; GI can vary by variety and ripeness.</p>

  <section id="howto" aria-labelledby="howto-h">
    <h2 id="howto-h">How to enjoy dates (the woke way)</h2>
    <div class="grid-3">
      <div class="feature">
        <h3>Snack plate</h3>
        <p>2 dates + a handful of almonds or walnuts. Add orange zest and a pinch of sea salt.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="feature">
        <h3>Stuffed energy bites</h3>
        <p>Fill with tahini/peanut butter, sprinkle sesame, chill. Dessert, but better.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="feature">
        <h3>Blend for sweetness</h3>
        <p>Soak briefly, then blend into smoothies or sauces instead of refined sugar.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="storage" aria-labelledby="storage-h">
    <h2 id="storage-h">Storage & safety</h2>
    <ul class="card">
      <li>Room temp for short term; refrigerate or freeze for longer freshness and to retain moisture.</li>
      <li>Look for intact skins; harmless ‚Äúsugar bloom‚Äù can appear as white crystals on the surface.</li>
      <li>Allergen-friendly fruit, but stuffed/flavored dates may contain nuts, sesame, or gluten from processing ‚Äî check labels.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section id="faq" aria-labelledby="faq-h">
    <h2 id="faq-h">FAQ</h2>
    <div class="faq" role="list">
      <details role="listitem">
        <summary>Do dates spike insulin?</summary>
        <div>
          <p>Whole dates have fiber and polyphenols that slow absorption. Studies place most dates in the low-to-medium GI range, and moderate portions haven‚Äôt shown harm to glycemic control ‚Äî but response varies by person and variety. See Sources.</p>
        </div>
      </details>
      <details role="listitem">
        <summary>How many dates are ‚Äúhealthy‚Äù per day?</summary>
        <div>
          <p>A reasonable snack portion is about 2 large or 3 small dates, especially if paired with protein/fat. If you track calories or glucose, fit them into your targets.</p>
        </div>
      </details>
      <details role="listitem">
        <summary>Are dates good for workouts?</summary>
        <div>
          <p>Yes ‚Äî they‚Äôre a quick source of carbs plus potassium. Pair with protein after training for recovery.</p>
        </div>
      </details>
      <details role="listitem">
        <summary>Are dates better than white sugar?</summary>
        <div>
          <p>For most goals, yes: dates are a whole food with fiber, minerals, and phytochemicals. They still add sugars and calories, so think ‚Äúbetter sweet,‚Äù not ‚Äúfree food.‚Äù</p>
        </div>
      </details>
    </div>
  </section>
